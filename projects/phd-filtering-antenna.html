Role: PhD Researcher (2020–2024)
Institution: University of Kent, United Kingdom
Supervisors: Prof. Steven Gao, Dr. Hanyang Wang

This research systematically explores the application of filtering antenna technology to compact terminal antenna systems, aiming to improve both spectral selectivity and mutual coupling suppression.

Key research outcomes include:

  • Establishing a unified synthesis method linking coupling-matrix filters with radiating structures.
  • Developing transversal filtering antennas (TFAs) that provide controllable transmission zeros for out-of-band suppression.
  • Proposing intrinsic decoupling mechanisms between closely spaced antennas without extra circuits.
  • Demonstrating prototype filtering–decoupling antennas with high isolation and wideband in-band matching, verified experimentally.

The work provides theoretical and practical foundations for integrated, spectrum-compliant multi-antenna systems in future terminal and base-station designs.
